# Customer Discovery Summary
 
## Project Title
Peer Tutoring Booking Platform
 
## Target Users Studied
Undergraduate students at Rangsit University, including both students who have needed academic help and students who have informally tutored peers before.
 
## Number of Responses / Interviews
8 short interviews (5 students who have sought tutoring help, 3 students who have tutored others informally) and 1 quick informal survey shared in a class group chat with 14 responses.
 
## Main Repeated Problem
Students consistently said they don't know who among their peers is actually good at a subject and willing to tutor. Most said they only find a tutor "by luck," through a friend mentioning someone, and several mentioned giving up on finding help entirely before an exam because they ran out of time searching.
 
## Main Current Workarounds
Most students said they ask in their class or year-group chat group and wait to see if anyone responds. A few mentioned asking a senior they already know personally. None of the interviewees had ever used a structured tool or app to find a tutor — everything was informal and relationship-based.
 
## Strongest Evidence Found
Several students independently described the same pain point without being prompted: not knowing in advance whether a potential tutor is actually free, qualified, or willing, which leads to wasted time messaging multiple people. Two students who had tutored informally also said they would have tutored more often if students could find them more easily, suggesting tutor supply may not be the main blocker.
 
## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
It is still unclear how many students would be willing to pay for tutoring versus expecting it for free, since responses were mixed and the question wasn't asked consistently across all interviews. It's also unclear how tutoring quality would be verified, since this wasn't raised by users but may matter once the platform is in use.
 
## What We Must Revise
The team should add a consistent question about willingness to pay (or expectation of free tutoring) across all future interviews, and explore how much trust/verification matters to users before they would book a session with an unfamiliar tutor.
 
## Decision for Next Lab
Continue with same problem direction
 
## Updated Problem Statement
Students struggle to find subject-specific tutors among their peers in a timely way, relying on informal, relationship-based methods (chat groups, asking friends) that often fail or take too long, especially close to exams — resulting in students going without help even when willing peer tutors are available elsewhere on campus.
