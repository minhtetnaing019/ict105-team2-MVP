# Customer Discovery Summary
 
## Project Title
Peer Tutoring Booking Platform
 
## Target Users Studied
Undergraduate students at Rangsit University, including both students who have needed academic help and students who have informally tutored peers before.
 
## Number of Responses / Interviews
8 short interviews (5 students who have sought tutoring help, 3 students who have tutored others informally) and 1 quick informal survey shared in a class group chat with 14 responses.
 
## Main Repeated Problem
Students consistently described the same recent experience: needing help in a subject close to an exam, messaging around to find a tutor, and not hearing back in time or not finding anyone qualified at all. Several said this happens almost every exam period, roughly 2-3 times per semester. On a severity scale of 1-5, most rated it a 4, explaining that the timing pressure near exams is what makes it serious rather than just inconvenient.
 
## Main Current Workarounds
Most students said they ask in their class or year-group chat group and wait to see if anyone responds. A few mentioned asking a senior they already know personally. None of the interviewees had ever used a structured tool or app to find a tutor — everything was informal and relationship-based. Several described this method as slow and unreliable, since there's no way to know who is actually free or willing until someone happens to reply.
 
## Strongest Evidence Found
Severity and consequence were consistent across respondents: students rated the problem 4/5 on average, and when asked what happens if it isn't solved, most said they either go into the exam unprepared or settle for a tutor they aren't confident in just to save time. Two students who had tutored informally also said they would tutor more often if students could find them more easily, suggesting tutor supply may not be the main blocker, demand-discovery is.
 
## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
It is still unclear how many students would be willing to pay for tutoring versus expecting it for free, since this question wasn't asked consistently across all interviews. It's also unclear how tutoring quality would be verified, since this wasn't raised by users but may matter once the platform is in use.
 
## What We Must Revise
The team should add a consistent question about willingness to pay (or expectation of free tutoring) across all future interviews, and explore how much trust or verification matters to users before they would book a session with an unfamiliar tutor.
 
## Decision for Next Lab
Continue with same problem direction
 
## Updated Problem Statement
Students struggle to find subject-specific tutors among their peers in time, relying on informal, relationship-based methods (chat groups, asking friends) that fail roughly 2-3 times per semester, most often near exams — a problem rated 4/5 in severity by affected students, who report either entering exams unprepared or settling for a tutor they are not confident in.
