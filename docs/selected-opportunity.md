# Selected Semester Project Opportunity
 
## Selected Project Title
Peer Tutoring Booking Platform
 
## Target User
Undergraduate students who need academic help in specific subjects, and students who are willing to tutor others in subjects they are strong in.
 
## Problem Statement
Students who struggle with a subject often do not know who among their peers is willing or qualified to tutor them, while students who are capable of tutoring have no easy way to make themselves known. This leads to wasted time, unmet academic needs, and missed opportunities for peer-to-peer learning.
 
## Current Alternative
Students currently rely on word-of-mouth, asking classmates directly, or posting informally in course or year-group chat groups. This reaches only a limited audience, has no structure for scheduling, and makes it hard to confirm a tutor's availability or subject expertise.
 
## Proposed IT Solution
A web-based platform where students who want to tutor can list the subjects they can teach along with their available time slots, and students who need help can browse these listings and book a session directly with a tutor.
 
## Why This Opportunity Was Selected
In the team's NUF evaluation, Peer Tutoring Booking Platform scored highest overall, with strong marks in usefulness and feasibility and a reasonable novelty score, since no dedicated peer tutoring tool currently exists on campus. Compared to other ideas such as the canteen status board or ride-share app, this project does not depend on real-time data accuracy from third parties (like vendors or drivers), making it more controllable and testable by the team within a single semester.
 
## Why This Is Feasible Without IoT or Advanced Cybersecurity
The platform does not require any physical sensors, hardware, or real-time device integration, so no IoT components are needed. User data involved (names, subjects, time slots, bookings) is low-risk and does not include sensitive personal or financial information, so the team can rely on basic authentication and standard web security practices rather than advanced cybersecurity measures. The entire MVP can be built using standard web development tools and a simple backend service.
 
## Possible MVP Type
HTML/CSS/JS prototype
 
## Initial Validation Plan for Lab 03
The team will ask a small sample of fellow students, including both students who have struggled to find a tutor and students who would be willing to tutor others, to gauge real interest in the platform. The team will collect evidence such as informal interview notes, a short survey on willingness to use the platform, and reactions to a basic mockup or wireframe of the booking flow. The key question the team must answer first is whether enough students are willing to act as tutors to make the platform's core matching function actually work in practice.
